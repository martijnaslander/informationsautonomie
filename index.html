The format is the message.

For 250 years, thinkers have circled the same problem without naming it. Buffon. Otlet. Vannevar Bush. McLuhan. Drucker. Karasek. Kahneman. Gloria Mark. David Allen. Clay Shirky.

None of them named the layer that was blocking the vision.

In this book, Martijn Aslander shows where all these lines converge — in a layer so ordinary it has become invisible: the file format.

When information is stored in formats that computers cannot read without the software that created them, autonomy becomes a slogan. Workers are asked to be self-directed while being structurally denied the informational conditions for self-direction. The cause is embedded in the format. The symptom appears in the body.

This is not a book against technology. It is a book against technological unconsciousness.

About the author

Martijn Aslander is a knowledge strategist, speaker and founder of the PKM Summit — an annual gathering of thinkers and practitioners in personal knowledge management. After a stroke forced months of stillness, he saw what decades of work had been circling: the file format as the root cause of organisational dysfunction. He has since tested this insight with government agencies, police forces and large organisations across Europe.

Information Autonomy is the result.
